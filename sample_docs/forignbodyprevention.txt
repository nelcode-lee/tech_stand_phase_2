

Responsibility
All Employees
All employees have a responsibility to minimize the risk of contamination from any foreign material contamination in our products to ensure that the risk of causing harm and/ or subsequent injury to the consumer and the business is minimized.  
•	Line leaders – Complete Pre-start up checks on all equipment. Assist in the investigation and close out of internal foreign material finds, and implementation of corrective actions.
•	Raw material Intake operative – All raw material and packaging inspection. Wooden pallets and Dolavs used for raw materials. Assist in the investigation and close out of internal foreign material finds, and implementation of corrective actions.
•	Food Safety Team- Complete GMP audits, identify and escalate any foreign material finds to department managers and assist in closing out any actions. Check all new batches of detectable pens prior to issue. Assist in the investigation and close out of internal foreign material finds.
•	Hygiene – Completing equipment surveys during cleaning activities and escalating any potential contamination risks to prevent product contamination occurring. Assist in the investigation and close out of internal foreign material finds, and implementation of corrective actions, purchase, storage & issue of Blue earplugs.
•	H&S/ Purchasing – Purchase and issuing of detectable pens, blue plasters and traceability tags. 
•	First Aiders – Control, issue and return of blue plasters

Frequency
•	At all times
	
Procedure
The integrity of the product MUST be maintained at all times.
Foreign bodies can originate from many areas within the business, including hair, metal, plastic, equipment parts, machinery, personal items, raw materials, string, elastic, tape, and food contact packaging. This list is not exhaustive. As a general rule, if something is not intended to be part of the product, it has the potential to become a foreign body if not properly controlled.
To help reduce the risk of foreign body contamination, all staff entering the site must follow the hygiene rules. If anyone sees something that could be a foreign body hazard, they should report it to their supervisor or manager straight away so it can be dealt with quickly. Any internal foreign body finds are treated as near misses and investigated by the site, with root cause and corrective actions put in place. Full details are in procedure FSP042, and customer complaints involving foreign bodies are handled according to customer complaints procedure.


Visitors/ Contractors 
If visitors or contractors need to bring equipment into production areas, they must:
1.	Record the Equipment
•	Log all items on the SINICA online system or form FSR004g.
•	Must be approved and condition checked by someone with override ability on sinica.
2.	Check for Damage
•	The site host must inspect the equipment before and after entering production.
•	If damaged before entry, the item cannot be taken into the factory.
•	If damaged after use, the area must be checked for contamination.
3.	Report Any Damage
•	If damage occurs, the equipment owner must inform their host or a site team member immediately.
•	If the visitor worked near a production line, the line must be stopped, and quarantine procedure FSP042 followed.
4.	Approval & Sign-Off
•	The host must confirm the equipment is safe to use and sign off before and after the visit.
•	If damage is found after the visit, it must be logged, and any affected product quarantined and investigated.


All Employees
To help prevent foreign body contamination:
•	Report any potential foreign body risks to your area manager immediately.
•	Do not bring personal items or unauthorised equipment into production areas.
•	Do not wear damaged clothing that could pose a risk.
•	Check all equipment used in production to make sure it’s intact and safe to use.
•	If any equipment or machinery is damaged, stop using it and report it so it can be isolated.
Regular checks will be carried out in all areas, including lockers and canteens. If unauthorised items are found, disciplinary action will be taken.

Production PPE
All PPE must be worn correctly at all times;
•	Ear protection is mandatory in low risk production areas, blue, detectable ear plugs are to ne worn underneath the mob cap.
•	Hair: Hair must be fully covered. If you have long hair, ask a colleague to check it’s properly tucked in.
•	Mob Cap: All food handlers must wear a colour-coded mob cap before putting on their overall. Never wear an overall without a mob cap.
•	Beard Snood: If you have stubble or beard growth, you must wear a beard snood or balaclava.
•	Coats: Coats must be fully fastened with the hood up. If a fastener is missing, return the coat to hygiene and get a replacement.
•	No Personal Clothing: Personal items like hoods or scarves must not be visible outside the coat.

Gloves/ Sleeves/ Aprons 
All production PPE which is used within the factory has the potential to cause a foreign body risk to products if not managed effectively and used in the correct manner.
1.	Only use factory-issued PPE (gloves, sleeves, aprons).
2.	If any item is ripped or damaged, dispose of it immediately and replace it.
3.	If any part of your PPE is missing, report it to your line leader or supervisor straight away.
4.	The Food Safety Team will investigate and log it as a near miss if needed.
5.	If damaged PPE is found but all parts are accounted for, it must still be disposed of and replaced.
6.	Any PPE brought onto site must be removed from outer packaging and placed in the correct holder.
7.	Remove elastic bands from PPE before entering production areas.

Product Contact Trays Utensils and Belts
1.	Trays
•	All trays are checked during cleaning.
•	Damaged trays must be removed from production and placed in a designated area for Hygiene and Engineering to inspect.
•	If you spot a damaged tray during work, report it to your line leader immediately.
•	Trays are also checked during GMP and foreign body audits.
2.	Utensils & Belts
•	Checked daily during start-up and clean-down by the Line Leader.
•	If you see any damage during production, report it immediately.
•	Damaged items must be removed from use.
•	If a belt is damaged, stop the line and inform Engineering. The line must not restart until the belt is fixed or replaced.
3.	Knives
•	Controlled using the fish knife issue sheet (FSR009bb) and Equipment check Sheet (FSR020b).
•	If your knife is damaged, report it to your line leader straight away.


Purchasing

•	When buying items for use in the factory, choose brightly coloured and detectable materials wherever possible to help prevent foreign body risks.
•	Staples are not allowed in production areas.
•	If a stapler is needed, request a staple-less stapler through your area or line manager.

Issue of Factory Pens and Plasters

Pens Policy
1.	Only company-issued X-ray detectable pens are allowed in factory areas.
2.	New pens require return of the old one ("new for old" policy).
3.	Pen batches must be X-ray tested:
•	Test 5 pens from each batch with a product that passed X-ray.
•	Record results on form Quor FSR036b.
•	If any pen fails, test the whole batch and return failures to supplier.
•	Approved pens are signed and dated by the FST before use.

Plasters Policy
1.	Only company-issued blue waterproof plasters are allowed in factory areas.
2.	Home plasters must be replaced with company ones before entering production.
3.	Plasters are issued by trained first aiders:
•	Each plaster is numbered and logged on FSR004l.
•	At shift end, plaster must be intact and signed off by a first aider or shift manager.
•	It’s the employee’s responsibility to report for plaster check at shift end.
4.	Plasters must be covered with a company glove in production areas.


Blue Ear plugs Policy
•	Xray detectable blue ear plugs must be worn, underneath the mob cap in all Low Risk production areas, these are available in dispensers in the low risk changing area.
•	The plastic bag containing the ear plugs should be disposed of in the waste bins provided.
•	It is the responsibility of the employee to discard any damaged ear plugs in the waste bin, inform their line leader or Manager and replace with new ones.
•	On a monthly basis, 5 sets of ear defenders must be sent through the Xrays to ensure they are detectable, this check is carried out & recorded by FST on Quor.

Lost Items
•	If a pen, ear defenders or plaster is lost, stop production immediately.
•	Inspect the area and inform the FST to assist with the inspection.

Site Wood Policy

1.	Wooden pallets are only allowed in:
2.	Goods intake storage areas (except the Vegetable chiller)
3.	Collation area
4.	Despatch
5.	All pallets must be in good condition – no damage allowed.
6.	No other wooden items (e.g. pencils, clipboards, tools, brushes) are allowed inside the factory.
7.	A site map showing wood-allowed areas is available on SharePoint under Site Plans → YB Site Map.





Cardboard:

1.	Cardboard is only allowed in production areas where approved and controlled, as described in:
•	Debagging & De-boxing Procedure (FSP026)
•	Cardboard Risk Assessment (RA009b)
2.	Where possible, remove all cardboard packaging in the intake area to reduce contamination risk.
3.	If cardboard must be used on site, make sure:
•	Damaged packaging is handled carefully
•	It is disposed of properly
•	It does not pose a risk to products

Glass, Perspex, Brittle Plastics and Ceramics

1.	All items made of glass, perspex, brittle plastic, or ceramics are listed by Technical on Quor and audited regularly (daily, weekly, or monthly) based on risk.
2.	If any such item breaks, stop production immediately and report it to Technical. Follow procedure FSP002.
3.	If you find damaged equipment, report it to your line leader straight away. They will inform Technical, who will assess the risk and decide what action to take.
4.	Glass is avoided where possible. If used, it must be protected.
5.	Factory lights must have shatter-resistant covers. If broken, follow the glass breakage procedure.
6.	Oven doors must have reinforced, protected glass.
7.	No unauthorised glass is allowed on site – including glass food containers in the canteen. See FSP002.

Metal Contamination Risk

1.	Daily Checks:
•	Metal sieves and filters used with raw materials must be checked daily.
•	If damaged or metal is found, stop production immediately and inform the Line Leader, Area Manager, and FST.
•	Follow quarantine procedure FSP042 and record on the Non-Conformance Log.
2.	Damaged Equipment:
•	If any equipment is damaged during checks or production, stop production and inform the Line Leader or Shift Manager.
•	FST will investigate and decide if quarantine is needed (FSP042).
•	Damaged equipment must:
•	Be quarantined
•	Have a works request form (FSR031a) completed
•	Be repaired by Engineering
•	Be cleaned by Hygiene
•	Be signed off by Engineering, Hygiene, Production, and FST before reuse
3.	Metal Utensils:
•	Items like knives, chainmail gloves, and scissors are controlled by Line Leaders/Supervisors.
•	Checked and logged on pre-start-up sheets each shift.
4.	Rack Loading Procedure:
•	Loading trays: Top down
•	Unloading trays: Bottom up
•	Prevents metal fragments from contaminating products
5.	Maintenance Checks:
•	All metal machinery is checked under the PPM schedule.
•	Ideally done outside production hours.
•	If done during production, equipment must be cleaned and signed off before reuse.
•	Glass, brittle plastic, or ceramic replacements must be done outside production hours and logged.
6.	X-ray Checks:
•	All products are X-rayed before dispatch (see procedure FSP048).
7.	Magnet Checks:
•	Magnets at veg inspection tables are checked after each bin.
•	If metal is found, stop production, inform QA, and follow FSP042.
•	Record on the Non-Conformance Log.
8.	Prohibited Items:
•	No staples, paper clips, or drawing pins allowed in the factory.

Intake 

1.	Every delivery of raw materials and packaging must be visually checked by intake staff for foreign body contamination.
2.	Check all pallets and Dolavs (wooden and plastic) for damage:
•	If damaged and product is at risk, inspect the product and replace the pallet/Dolav.
•	Isolate the damaged item and contact the supplier for collection.
3.	If severe damage is found during unloading:
•	Stop unloading
•	Check all products for contamination
•	If contamination is found, inform the FST or Area Manager immediately
•	Contaminated product must not be used
4.	The Area Manager will:
•	Assess the risk
•	Either reject the load or remove contaminated items and isolate them
5.	If no contamination is found, transfer product to a safe pallet/Dolav to prevent future risk.
6.	If the load is rejected:
•	Complete a Product Non-Conformance Report
•	Send it to the supplier with issue details and response deadline
•	Quarantine and return the rejected load

Ingredient Tubs/Containers 
1.	All ingredient tubs which raw materials are delivered in must be single use and under no circumstances be used within production.  
2.	Work in progress trays/tubs will be inspected before each use (e.g. after clean) for signs of damage, any damaged tubs/containers will be removed and placed on hold and logged on the Quarantine / On Hold log

Raw material packaging

1.	Packaging must be:
•	A contrasting colour to the product
•	The correct gauge/material to avoid contamination risk
2.	Check all products before processing to ensure no product is trapped in packaging.
3.	All raw materials and packaging must come from approved suppliers.
4.	Finished product packaging must be from an approved supplier.
5.	Re-work or WIP packaging must:
•	Be a contrasting colour
•	Have the correct gauge/tensile strength
6.	Remove all polythene wrapping from butter, cheese, meat, etc. before cutting or mincing.
7.	If only part of an ingredient bag is used:
•	Loosely knot or heat seal the bag
•	Do not use tape, sticky labels, or cable ties
8.	Shrink wrap may be used to stabilise loads:
•	Use coloured wrap if needed
•	Wrap to minimise tearing

Packaging Quality Checks 

1.	Quarterly checks are done on polythene liners and bags using a micrometre to make sure they match the specification.
2.	The specification and documents are reviewed to confirm:
•	Packaging meets customer standards
•	Has physical strength
•	Complies with legislation
3.	If packaging is not correct:
•	Quarantine the packaging/products (follow FSP042)
•	Raise a supplier non-conformance
•	The Technical Manager or deputy will decide whether to release or return the stock


Cleaning Utensils:

1.	Use equipment only for its intended purpose
– e.g. don’t use a squeegee like a brush.
2.	If any cleaning tool is damaged during use or before use:
•	Remove it from production immediately
•	Give it to the Hygiene team for replacement
3.	Daily checks for damage are done by the Hygiene Manager or deputy.
4.	If damaged equipment is found at any other time:
•	Report it to the FST
•	It will be logged on the Non-Conformance Log
•	Then removed, replaced, and handled by the Hygiene team

Area Line leaders 

1.	Team Responsibility
•	Make sure all team members know they are responsible for the equipment they use.
2.	Equipment Checks
•	Check all equipment before issuing and after use.
•	Includes: conveyor belts, nuts & bolts, chainmail gloves, trays, knives, PPE, pens, ladles, sieves, QAS sheets, etc.
3.	Start-Up Checks
•	Complete start-up checks before production begins.
•	If an issue is found, report to the Area Manager.
•	If the risk is high, do not use the equipment until fixed.
•	Temporary fixes must not pose contamination risks.
•	All actions must be logged on the Non-Conformance Log.
4.	Ingredient Checks
•	Ensure all dry and liquid ingredients are sieved/filtered correctly (see FSP023).
•	Ensure de-bagging and de-boxing is done by trained staff only (see FSP026).


Equipment control 

1.	Processing equipment is stored in the production office.
•	Check it against the inventory list when issuing and returning.
•	Sign the log to confirm everything is present and undamaged.
2.	If any equipment is damaged:
•	Do not use it
•	Inform your Area Manager immediately
•	It must be removed and replaced
3.	If any equipment is missing from the box:
•	Inform your Area Manager
•	An investigation will be started to find it
4.	If you find extra equipment in the box that’s not on the inventory:
•	Hand it in to your Line Leader or Area Manager
📄 For full details, see Equipment Procedure FSP020


New equipment

1.	Before using new equipment in the factory:
•	A sample or full review must be done by site management
•	This ensures there’s no risk of foreign body contamination
2.	After installation:
•	The Glass & Hard Plastic Register must be reviewed and updated


Internal Foreign material finds 

1.	If a foreign body is found during production:
•	Stop the line immediately
•	Try to identify the source of contamination
•	Communicate with other areas if needed (e.g. pastry team if found in sausage roll)
2.	Report all foreign body finds to:
•	FST and/or Engineers for full investigation
•	FST, Managers, and Engineers must help with the investigation and corrective actions
3.	Complete form FSR009ja for every internal foreign body find.
4.	Report the find immediately in the ‘Internal Foreign Body Finds’ Teams chat
•	Include all relevant details
•	By end of day, log it on the Foreign Body Tracker for trending
5.	Emerging Patterns:
•	If 2 or more of the same foreign body are found in one run:
•	Report as a non-conformance on Quor
•	Inform FTM & FM
•	If 2 or more foreign bodies are found during High Risk packing:
•	Continue the run
•	Place product on hold in collation
•	Log hold on Quor & NAV
•	FTM or STM will decide next steps after investigation
