# Human Findings

## CLEANSOR – Clarity, Readability, Plain Language Improvements

- Replace difficult phrasing such as "adhered" with simpler words like "followed".
- Use "quantity" instead of "how much product is required".
- Seek to clarify the sentence: "on the day of outload it details the quantity of products required by type and by depot location". (because I am not sure what this means myself!)
- Clarify a common term for pallet, dolly, dolav, rack as all these can be outload equipment. **LOGISTIC UNIT**
- Rather than use of "old", stock rotation wording should be simplified to:
  - "Pick stock in correct rotation. The CMEX system ensures rotation is followed."
- Replace "next product to be picked…" repetition with a clearer step such as:
  - "Repeat the process for each remaining product until the order is complete."
- Remove ambiguous language such as "appropriate stock".
- Clarify terms such as "closed", "new vehicle", "load documents", "pallet sheet". Should we start writing a glossary to train the agents on, especially on common language that might carry a specific meaning?

---

## SPECIFIER – Precision, Definitions, Measurability

- Replace vague concepts such as "appropriate stock" with a defined meaning; or can this be removed altogether to improve the operatives choice and not put in a procedural step that may or may not be followed.
- "Build a pallet" should be replaced with the more accurate term: "Create a *standard language of equipment* for despatch."
- Specify how a new pallet/dolly number is generated.
- Define "closed pallet/dolly": is it when fully packed, when wrapped, or when the Despatch operative completes it?
- Define terminology in a glossary, including:
  - SSCC (Serial Shipping Container Code), how it is printed, and where it is attached (front or side – can't be both).
- Enumerate "A" SSCC – is this one or two or more?
- Clarify whether the SSCC must be applied by the person picking the dolly or by Despatch.
- Clarify the parameters for vehicle temperature checks (e.g., use "less than" or "greater than" in full words, not symbols).
- Where "new vehicle" is referenced, specify meaning, e.g.,
  - "A vehicle newly arriving to site for loading or is this the start of a new load"

---

## SEQUENCER – Order, Flow, Step Logic

- Improve sequencing to ensure product dispatch steps flow logically from demand, picking, dolly building, wrapping, labelling, quality checks, loading, and documentation.
- Step 4 should be simplified and clearly explain its intent.
- Ensure sequencing avoids contradictions such as:
  - The manifest being signed before loading.
  - Temperature checks only on the "last dolly", which would require full unloading if out of specification.
  - Consider amends: "Ensure three dollies/pallets are checked before loading begins and record temperature."
- Clarify sequencing for vehicle checks:
  - Vehicle condition & suitability and cleanliness
  - Removal of debris if required
  - Temperature validation
  - Loading
- Ensure checking of labels and TELs occurs at picking, not at loading.
- Clarify the process for "load label creation" and link to any relevant SOP.

---

## CONFLICTOR – Contradictions, Inconsistencies

- The document refers to "pallets & dollies", "loads, trailers & vehicles" interchangeably. Standardise terms.
- The statement "food cannot be carried on a vehicle carrying non food items" is incorrect, as empty dollies are non food items and are routinely carried.
- Statement that "the load is free of debris/glass/etc." is inaccurate; this applies to the vehicle, not the product.
- "vehicle temperature can drop before loading" – vehicle can drop to the required temperature before loading.
- The manifest signature sequence contradicts operational reality: the driver should sign after loading. All three documents should be signed.

---

## TERMINATOR – Terminology, Consistency, Glossary

- Replace all references to "pallet" with "dolly", unless the product is genuinely shipped on pallets.
- Standardise terminology for:
  - Dollies
  - Load sheet / manifest
  - Trailer vs vehicle
  - SSCC label
  - TEL (Tray End Label)
- Ensure all terms appear in a glossary, including:
  - SSCC
  - Dolly
  - Load sheet / Manifest
  - Pallet sheet / dolly sheet
  - Stock rotation
- Clarify which documents are "load documents" and whether this is synonymous with "manifest".

---

## RISK ASSESSOR – Missing Information, Failure Points

- No procedure exists for Unloading – significant omission.
- Lack of clarity on what to do if a vehicle is unsuitable (dirty, damaged, temperature out of range). Add explicit corrective actions.
- If temperature is out of tolerance on the last dolly loaded, current method forces complete unloading. Add preventative step (e.g., random checks before loading).
- Lack of guidance on whether seals are required for all trailers only or also for vehicles, and what document records this (specific document reference).

---

## VALIDATOR – Compliance, BRC, Food Safety

- Ensure compliance with BRC regarding:
  - Vehicle hygiene checks
  - Temperature monitoring
  - Seal verification
  - Documentation of checks, including record IDs
- Trailer suitability wording must reflect BRC expectations:
  - Vehicle "free from debris, glass, pests, signs of damp".
  - Equipment must be capable of maintaining temperature, but site can only verify that the vehicle is operating at correct temperature at arrival.
- Load documents should carry clear document control identifiers, including version, date, and reviewer.
- Ensure that "food vs non food segregation" reflects practical rules: empty dollies are permitted.

---

## FORMATTER – Structure, Presentation

- Document is titled "Loading and Unloading Procedure", yet includes no unloading instructions.
- The scope states dispatch to customers only, but dispatch to third party storage or other businesses is not included.
- Frequency section refers only to "loading"; unloading frequency should also be stated.
- Structure should separate:
  - Picking
  - Dolly creation
  - Wrapping and labelling
  - Vehicle checks
  - Loading
  - Documentation
  - Sealing and release
  - Unloading (missing)
- Replace dense text with clear, sequential bullet points.

---

## General Omissions (Cross Agent)

- No section on unloading at all, despite this being part of the stated procedure.
- Product returns should not be a part of this procedure.
- No guidance on SOP linkages (e.g., load label creation).
- Inconsistent language across the document (pallet/dolly, trailer/vehicle, load/manifest).
- No clarification of document control for vehicle seal tags.
- No defined process for CMEX update confirming full order completion.
